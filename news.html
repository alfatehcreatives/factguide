Policy Shift

IRCC Tightens Canada's Remote Work Exemptions

Canada's Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) has finalized a pivot in its handling of digital nomads entering the country as temporary visitors. New operational bulletins mandate that immigration officers scrutinize the source of an applicant's income more rigorously. The primary concern is preventing the "creeping labor participation" of remote workers who may be leveraging tourist status to perform work that affects the domestic labor market. Applicants are now strongly advised to carry proof of foreign employment, corporate certification, and an explicit itinerary that confirms their intent to reside temporarily without seeking Canadian employer sponsorship.

Border Tech

EES Biometric System: Operational Reality

The European Union has moved beyond the pilot phase of the Entry/Exit System (EES), fully replacing the manual passport stamp for non-EU travelers. This automated digital system captures facial images and fingerprints upon entry to the Schengen Area, creating a consolidated log of every crossing. While the system is designed to accelerate border throughput long-term, the initial transition has caused significant processing bottlenecks at major aviation and rail hubs, notably in Frankfurt and Madrid. Travelers should expect a one-time "registration delay" on their first visit, as biometrics must be recorded in the centralized security database.

Visa Update

Japan Launches Targeted Nomad Program

Japan has officially opened its highly anticipated digital nomad visa program, positioning itself as a premier destination for high-earning remote professionals. The visa allows for a six-month stay, specifically targeting individuals with an annual income exceeding ¥10 million (approx. $65,000+ USD). Unlike standard tourist permits, this program offers a clear legal framework for residing in Japan without being tied to a local employer, provided the remote work remains purely digital. The government is using this initiative to attract global tech talent to Tokyo and Fukuoka's burgeoning startup ecosystems.
